title: Factoring a quadratic polynomial in standard form
math: \\(ax^2+bx+c\\)
topic: factors
---

$$
\begin{align*}
(qx+r)(sx+t)&=(qx)(sx)+(qx)(t)+(r)(sx)+(r)(t)\\
&=qsx^2+qtx+rsx+rt\\
&=(qs)x^2+(qt+sr)x+rt
\end{align*}
$$

$$ax^2+bx+c=(qx+r)(sx+t)$$

if and only if

$$
\begin{align*}
a&=qs\\
b&=qt+sr\\
c&=rt
\end{align*}
$$

Let 

$$
m = qt
$$

and

$$
n = sr.
$$

Then

$$mn=ac,\qquad m+n=b$$

and

$$
\begin{align*}
ax^2+bx+c&=ax^2+mx+nx+c\\
&=(qs)x^2+qtx+srx+rt\\
&=qx(sx+t)+r(sx+t)\\
&=(qx+r)(sx+t).
\end{align*}
$$

If $$mn=ac$$ and $$m+n=b$$ then $$n=b-m$$ and so 

$$m(b-m)=ac$$

i.e.

$$m^2-bm+ac=0$$
